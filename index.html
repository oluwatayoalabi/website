Oluwatayo Alabi

I am currently a second-year PhD Student/Research Assistant in Sen's Laboratory at the The Department of Biology and Biochemistry, University of Houston, Texas, where I work on understanding how cell-surface adhesion protein integrin aid cell communication by providing a point of anchorage to the extracellular matrix (ECM). senior staff research scientist at Google Research, where I work on computer vision and machine learning.

At Google I've worked on Glass, Lens Blur, HDR+, Jump, Portrait Mode, Portrait Light, and NeRF. I did my PhD at UC Berkeley, where I was advised by Jitendra Malik and funded by the NSF GRFP. I've received the C.V. Ramamoorthy Distinguished Research Award and the PAMI Young Researcher Award.

Research

I'm interested in the role of integrin in the metastasis of cancer cells as well as in the migration of cancer-fighting immune cells in tumor development. Much of my work at present is about understanding the dynamics of integrin signalling. Representative papers are highlighted.
